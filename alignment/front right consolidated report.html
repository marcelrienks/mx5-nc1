02 — Key Findings
CAMBER: Maximum achievable camber is −7mm (−0.85°) — 2mm / 0.25° short of the −9mm target — the factory eccentric bolt range is exhausted at front=+6. Aftermarket eccentric bolts with a larger lobe radius are required to reach −1.1°.
CASTER: The rear bolt is the dominant caster variable. With the front bolt locked at +2, measured caster spans 4.48° (rear=−6) to 7.78° (rear=+6). The 5.0° target interpolates to rear≈−4.
CONFLICT: The rear bolt simultaneously affects both camber and caster. Setting rear=−6 maximises camber contribution (−7mm with front=+6) but gives only 4.48° caster. Setting rear=−4 hits the 5.0° caster target at the cost of ≈0.5mm of camber (≈−2.5mm vs −3mm at rear=−6, both with front=+2; front=+6 not independently measurable).
RECOMMENDATION: Set front bolt → +6 and rear bolt → −4 to prioritise the on-target caster while accepting a camber shortfall that is inherent to the factory bolt design. Re-measure and fine-tune rear bolt one segment at a time to confirm 5.0° caster.
